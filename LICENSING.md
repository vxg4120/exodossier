## Why MIT

MIT is permissive by design: the goal of this project is **scientific adoption, collaboration, and
credit** — contribution and co-authorship, not commercialization. A permissive license maximizes the
chance that catalog maintainers, follow-up working groups (PHT / VSG / TFOP), and other researchers
can freely use, reproduce, and build on the reconciliation engine and its outputs. That openness is
the point; there is nothing here to fence off.

## Data (not code)

No raw catalog data is licensed or redistributed here. This repository ships **code and derived
aggregates/reports only — never raw catalog dumps.** The NASA Exoplanet Archive tables are a
U.S. Government work (public domain; cite the dataset DOI); ExoFOP-TESS requires attribution. See the
*Data sources and attribution* section of the [README](README.md). MIT covers this project's code and
outputs, not the underlying third-party datasets.
